💡 Fleet Insurance Guide
How to get the best coverage
🏆 How to reduce your fleet premium
📚 Data sources: ABI (Association of British Insurers) Fleet Insurance Report 2024 · Zurich FleetSmart Commercial Vehicle Survey 2024 · BVRLA Fleet Industry Guide 2024 · ICCT Fleet Electrification Report 2024 · LexisNexis Risk Solutions EV Insurance Index 2024 · Allianz Commercial Fleet Trends 2024
1. Fleet multi-vehicle policy — 5+ vehicles on a single policy typically saves 15–30% vs individual policies. Insurers include Zurich FleetSmart, AXA Fleet, Aviva Fleet.

2. Telematics / black box — fitting tracking devices can reduce premiums 10–25%. Insurers use driving behaviour data. Most major fleet insurers offer this.

3. Named driver vs any driver — named driver policies cost 20–40% less. Only viable if you have a stable driver roster.

4. Annual payment — paying annually instead of monthly saves typically 8–12% interest charges.

5. Excess management — raising your voluntary excess lowers the premium. For a fleet, self-insuring small claims (<$1,500) often saves money in aggregate.

6. Renewal benchmarking — fleet insurance should be tendered every 2–3 years minimum. Loyalty rarely pays — get at least 3 quotes each renewal cycle.
⚡ EV-Specific Insurance Notes
EV premiums are 20–35% higher than equivalent ICE vehicles (LexisNexis Risk Solutions 2024). Reasons:

• Higher vehicle value → higher replacement cost
• Battery repair/replacement: $8,000–$20,000
• Fewer specialist repairers → higher labour cost
• Evolving claims data → conservative pricing

How to reduce EV insurance costs:
• Choose insurers with dedicated EV products (e.g. Zurich EV Fleet, Aviva EV, Direct Line EV Fleet)
• Ensure charging infrastructure is covered (cable theft, charger damage)
• Check battery coverage — some policies exclude gradual degradation
• For PHEV: confirm both ICE and EV modes are covered equally
• Ask about agreed value vs market value — EV depreciation is faster than ICE in some segments

Battery replacement risk flag: If a BEV in your fleet is over 8 years old or has >160,000 km, battery replacement cost ($8k–$20k) should factor into your replacement vs repair decision. gofleetzy flags this in the score.
